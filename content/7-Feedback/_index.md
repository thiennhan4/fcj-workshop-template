- Looking back on nearly three months of interning at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)**, I feel that what I gained was not only knowledge about AWS and Cloud Computing but also a significant change in the way I learn and approach technical problems. Before joining the program, I thought learning Cloud mainly meant studying each service individually. However, throughout the internship, I realized that building a complete system requires connecting many different components together while always thinking from the perspective of a product developer.

- The internship journey was not always smooth. There were times when I spent a great deal of time reading documentation, experimenting with different approaches, or troubleshooting issues while deploying the system. Those experiences helped me grow and understand that learning technology is not only about the final result but also about the persistence required to find the right solution.


### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
- FCAJ provides an open, friendly, welcoming, and energetic learning environment and community. What impressed me the most was not only how approachable everyone was, but also how dedicated they were to supporting students throughout their learning journey. From warmly welcoming interns on the first day to helping them overcome difficulties by sharing knowledge and offering practical solutions, everyone was always willing to help. The Community Day and Meetup events clearly reflected this spirit. The atmosphere was both professional and friendly. During the Meetups, we had opportunities to interact with professionals in the industry and learn from the knowledge and experiences they shared. These events truly motivated learners to continue pursuing their Cloud journey.


**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
- Throughout the internship, the mentors closely followed each team's progress and were always willing to help whenever I encountered difficulties. What I appreciated the most was that they never created unnecessary pressure but instead listened carefully to the challenges interns were facing.

- Instead of providing step-by-step instructions, they often asked guiding questions or recommended appropriate resources for further research. This mentoring approach encouraged me to become more proactive in learning and helped me gain a deeper understanding rather than simply memorizing the steps.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
- One of the highlights of the program was that the learning content was always closely connected to real-world practice. During the project, I experienced the complete process of building a system, from brainstorming ideas and designing the architecture to deployment and testing.

- This experience was completely different from simply completing small assignments at university. It helped me understand that building a reliable application requires considering many important factors such as scalability, security, cost optimization, and long-term system operation.


**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
The biggest improvement I noticed after completing the program was my increased confidence.

- Previously, I often hesitated to explore new technologies because I thought they would be too difficult. Now, I have developed the habit of starting with the official documentation, experimenting step by step, and patiently resolving any issues that arise.

- Besides technical knowledge, I also improved my skills in work planning, time management, and presenting project outcomes after each development phase.


**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The activities organized by FCAJ were not limited to technical learning but also created valuable opportunities for interns to connect with professionals working in the Cloud industry.

- Through these networking sessions, I gained a better understanding of industry expectations, the skills required by employers, and current technology trends. These insights were extremely valuable because they are not something students often have the opportunity to learn in the classroom.


**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
In my opinion, FCAJ is an excellent program for students who want to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical experience.

- What I appreciated the most was that the program did not emphasize finishing tasks as quickly as possible. Instead, it encouraged interns to understand the purpose and underlying concepts behind every task they performed. This allowed me to learn much more than simply completing a checklist of assignments.

### Additional Questions
- What did you find **most satisfying** during your internship?  
    - What I remember most throughout the internship was not the day I completed the project, but the process of gradually overcoming every challenge I encountered.

    - There were times when I spent an entire afternoon searching for the root cause of an issue, reading documentation, and trying different solutions before finally resolving it. Looking back, those experiences helped me better understand how AWS services work and made me much more confident when facing similar problems in the future.

    - In addition, I truly appreciated the opportunity to work in an environment where everyone was always willing to share their experiences. The feedback and advice from the mentors helped me recognize both my strengths and the areas where I still need to improve.

- What do you think the company **should improve** for future interns?  
Based on my personal experience, I have a few suggestions that I believe could make the program even better:

    - Provide more hands-on exercises that simulate real-world scenarios commonly encountered by businesses so interns can further develop their troubleshooting skills.
    - Organize additional career development sessions, including CV writing and interview preparation for students approaching graduation.
    - Expand the learning topics to include more content on system security, AWS cost optimization, and post-deployment system operations.
    - Increase networking activities between different internship cohorts so participants can learn from and support one another.

- If recommending to a friend, would you **suggest they intern here**? Why or why not?  
    - Yes
    - If I have the opportunity, I would definitely recommend FCAJ to students who are interested in Cloud Computing or looking for an environment where they can develop practical skills.
    - In my opinion, the greatest value of the program is not simply the amount of knowledge it provides, but the way it helps learners develop the mindset of self-learning, independent research, and proactive problem-solving. These are essential skills for anyone entering the professional workplace.
